##  Business Overview

Oswal Pumps manufactures:

- Solar water pumps  
- Non-solar pumps (0.5 HP to 100 HP)  
- Electric motors and components  

The company follows a **backward integrated model**, helping it maintain strong margins.

Major revenue currently comes from:
- PM KUSUM Scheme  
- State-level projects (like Maharashtra schemes)

---

##  Growth Drivers

### 1. Government Push (PM KUSUM)

India is promoting solar irrigation:
- Reduces farmer electricity cost  
- Supports renewable energy  

This has been a key growth driver so far.

---

### 2. Diversification Strategy (Important)

The company is **not fully dependent on government schemes** and is actively working on:

- Expanding **exports**  
- Growing **solar EPC + component segment**  
- Strengthening **non-subsidy business**  

 As highlighted in concalls, management is focusing on reducing dependency over time.

---

### 3. Strong Execution

- 90,000+ pumps deployed  
- Fast execution (120–150 days)  
- Strong order pipeline  

---

### 4. Margin Advantage

- In-house manufacturing (backward integration)  
- Better cost control  
- ~18% PAT margins

###  Risks

1. Government Dependency (Still High)  
   Current revenue largely from government schemes  

2. Execution Risk in Diversification  
   New segments may take time to scale  

3. Payment Delays  
   Receivable cycle is 150+ days  

4. Tender-Based Business  
   Low pricing power  

5. State Concentration  
   Heavy reliance on Maharashtra  

6. Policy Risk  
   Growth depends on government continuation

##  Reality Check

FY27 projections assume **50% growth**,  
but management long-term guidance is **30–35% CAGR**.

 FY27 is a **bull case**, not guaranteed outcome.
